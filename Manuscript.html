Deriving Mean and Variance

The majority of articles report their summary statistics as either median and IQR1 and IQR3 or median and median absolute deviation (MAD), which will require an estimated conversion to mean and variance. I will also acknowledge that often median and MAD is given in cases where N(0,1) is not observed, but the problem is that median and MAD are not parameters of any known distribution. This is not an uncommon problem for those conducting meta-analyses to encounter which has created a plethora of well validated statistical methods of estimating mean and variance from limited summary statistics information given within an article.
